GDPR Compliance

Last updated: March 04, 2026

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that regulates the use of personal data of EU residents. OpeninYoutube is committed to GDPR compliance across all our services.

1. Data Protection Principles

We adhere to the following principles when processing personal data:

  • Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency: Data is processed legally and transparently.
  • Purpose limitation: Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes.
  • Data minimization: We collect only the data necessary for the intended purpose.
  • Accuracy: We take steps to ensure data is kept up to date.
  • Storage limitation: Data is kept only as long as necessary.
  • Integrity and confidentiality: Data is processed securely to protect against unauthorized access.

2. Rights of Data Subjects

Under GDPR, individuals have specific rights regarding their personal data. These include:

  • The right to be informed: Being told how your data is used.
  • The right of access: Requesting a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • The right to rectification: Asking us to correct inaccurate data.
  • The right to erasure: Asking us to delete your data (the "right to be forgotten").
  • The right to restrict processing: Limiting how we use your data.
  • The right to data portability: Receiving your data in a machine-readable format.
  • The right to object: Objecting to the use of your data for specific purposes.

3. Information We Process

OpeninYoutube primarily processes YouTube URLs provided by users to generate deep links. We do not require account registration or personal identity information for our core converter service. Any log data collected is used solely for service optimization and security purposes as outlined in our Privacy Policy.

4. Data Transfers

As a global service, data may be processed on servers located outside of the European Economic Area (EEA). We ensure that any such transfers are conducted in compliance with GDPR requirements, utilizing standard contractual clauses or other approved mechanisms to ensure a high level of data protection.

If you have any questions regarding our GDPR compliance or wish to exercise any of your data subject rights, please contact our Data Protection Office:
